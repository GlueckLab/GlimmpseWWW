Means: Introduction

In power analysis, it is necessary to choose mean values for the outcomes before running the study. The means are often obtained from published results or pilot data. Larger mean differences are easier to detect, and so have greater power. To detect smaller differences, the investigator will typically need a larger sample size.

To detect a difference between the groups, at least two subgroups should have means which differ by a scientifically meaningful amount. For example, in a study of cholesterol-lowering medication, we would expect the mean cholesterol level in the active drug group to be lower than the placebo group.

Model: Introduction

A model is used to examine relationships between variables in the study design. When defining the model, the investigator must consider several questions.

  • Clustering: Are the participants organized into groups (geographic regions, schools, etc.) which induce correlation?
  • Predictors and Groups: What factors distinguish independent participants or groups of participants?
  • Covariates: Is there an additional variable which is not of scientific interest, but still explains some of the variability in the outcome?
  • Responses: What outcomes are being measured in the study?
  • Repeated Measures: Are the outcomes observed once, at multiple times, or under multiple conditions?
  • Relative group sizes: Are there equal or unequal numbers of participants in each subgroup in the study?
  • Smallest group size: If calculating power, how many independent participants or groups of participants will be recruited in each study subgroup?

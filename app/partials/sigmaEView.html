Covariance of Errors: $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_e$ Matrix

For each independent sampling unit, $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_e$ is the covariance of the random errors. For univariate designs, $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_e$ will be a $(1\times1)$ matrix containing the variance of the error term. More complex structures may be entered for multivariate or repeated measures designs. Values for $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_e$ are typically obtained from pilot data or previous studies.

$\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_e$ is a square, symmetric matrix with dimensions equal to the number of columns in $\boldsymbol{B}$.
